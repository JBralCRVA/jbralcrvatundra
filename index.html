The Tundra

The Tundra biome is the coldest biome, which means it isn't good for human life. Tundra comes from the Finnish word tunturia, meaning treeless plain. This biome is known for it's low temperatures, little precipatation, and poor nutrients. Dead organic material provides the area with most of it's nutrients. The two most promnent nutrients in a Tundra is nitrogen and phosphorus. There are two different types of Tundras, an Articic Tundra and an Alphine Tundra.
